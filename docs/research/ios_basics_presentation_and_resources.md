### Lesson 4 - Multiple Gestures
Your device isn’t for just tapping!
There’s multiple ways to interact with the screen:
- Tapping (press down and immediately release - kinda like how they tell you **not** to “tap” an aquarium)
- Press and hold (like holding down a power button) - this can start an “edit mode” we’ll talk about later 
- Press, hold, and drag (in edit mode, used to move icons around; also used on keyboard for speed typing)
- **pinch-in and pinch-out** 
	- Used for zooming in and out
	- This is what Apple [officially calls](https://developer.apple.com/library/archive/documentation/WindowsViews/Conceptual/UIScrollView_pg/ZoomZoom/ZoomZoom.html) it (or at least used to as of [2015](https://english.stackexchange.com/a/177080))
- Tap with multiple fingers
	- I have a setting on my phone where I can tap three fingers three times and a special dialogue will appear to help me choose display “filters”, such as lowering the brightness below its normal minimum. 
For almost everything, context matters! Doing one gesture on the home screen doesn’t mean that same thing will happen inside an app.

### Lesson 6 - Accessibility
In the Settings app, there are many different ways to make your phone easier to use:
- [Assistive Access](https://www.theseniorlist.com/cell-phones/assistive-access/)
	- Larger icons; different customization options for apps.
	- May be seriously worth considering if using your phone has gotten difficult!
- VoiceOver - will describe things in real time about what your phone is showing on screen; very customizable with many different voices and slower or faster speeds
- Display and text size - Many options to make text and images on your phone easier to see
- Spoken Content - kind of like VoiceOver, but only reading the words on a webpage.
- AssistiveTouch
	- helps with using the phone if you have issues touching the screen
	- Provides a single button that shows a menu to help do "things" on your phone
	- Honestly, quite helpful to me to explain what "things" you can do, since I usually just have to call it what I "think" it is, not what Apple calls it.
- Hearing Devices
	- Pair with hearing aids and sound processors
- Add subtitles
- Many more things!

### Lesson 7 - Some Fun Stuff
- [**Tips App**](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/tips/id1069509450)
	- Can provide useful information about how to use your phone
	- Best part, will be updated for you phone's features! 
- [**Voice Dictation**](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/dictate-text-iph2c0651d2/ios)
	- Basically anywhere the keyboard appears, there is a tiny microphone icon at the bottom right
	- Click it, wait for the tone, and then you can start talking to write instead of typing!
	- Can even recognize some [formatting](https://support.apple.com/guide/iphone/commands-for-dictating-text-iph3bf19d7b9/ios), like "new line"
- [Shazam built in to phone](https://support.apple.com/en-us/118570)
	- Can add to control center, activate when listening to something out in public, and will tell you song name and artist!
