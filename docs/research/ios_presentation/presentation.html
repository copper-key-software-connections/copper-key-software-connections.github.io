Lesson 1 - Off/on and emergency calling

  • Do you know how to turn it on and off?
    • Press and hold the Side button and Volume Up button, then slide to power off.
    • Or press the side button five times in a row, and it will open the same slider screen.
  • Some issues can be helped by turning it fully off and on.
  • Emergency calling
    • Same procedure to bring up power off screen, but slide the SOS (Emergency Call) slider from left to right.

Lesson 2 - Everything is searchable

  • Look for the magnifying glass 🔎; will open a search bar you can type into.
  • Go to Settings app; there’s a search bar
  • Go to Notes; there’s a search bar
  • On home screen; there’s a search bar, and it will search your phone, the App Store, AND online!
  • On home screen, pull down, and a search bar appears!

Lesson 3 - Siri can do a lot of different things

  • Who here knows about Siri?
    • Voice assistant on iOS
  • A few ways to activate; mine has “Hey Siri” voice activation enabled.
  • Try it out:
    • “Set a timer for 10 seconds”
    • “Text <person name>”
    • When should I plant tomatoes in Mississippi?”
    • Almost any question you have!

Lesson 4 - Multiple Gestures

  • Your device isn’t for just tapping!
  • There’s multiple ways to interact with the screen:
    • Tapping (press down and immediately release - kinda like how they tell you not to “tap” an aquarium)
    • Press and hold (like holding down a power button) - this can start an “edit mode” we’ll talk about later
    • Press, hold, and drag (in edit mode, used to move icons around; also used on keyboard for speed typing)

Lesson 4 - Multiple Gestures (cont.)

  • pinch-in and pinch-out
    • Used for zooming in and out
    • This is what Apple officially calls it (or was in 2015)
  • Tap with multiple fingers
    • Used for some accessibility features, like double-tap with three fingers to zoom in on the screen (see customization and accessibility resources for more info!)
  • For almost everything, context matters!
    • Doing one gesture on the home screen doesn’t mean that same thing will happen inside an app.

Lesson 5 - Customization

  • You can customize many things on your phone:
    • Ringtone and text-tone
      • Including different sounds for different people!
    • App placement (and bottom “pins” - always shown)
    • Add widgets to home screen
    • Wallpaper (both locked and unlocked!)
    • Control center (a bit advanced)
    • More examples from Apple’s Support page!

Lesson 6 - Accessibility

  • In the Settings app, there are many different ways to make your phone easier to use:
    • Assistive Access
      • Larger icons; different customization options for apps.
      • May be seriously worth considering if using your phone has gotten difficult!
    • VoiceOver - will describe things in real time about what your phone is showing on screen; very customizable with many different voices and slower or faster speeds
    • Display and text size - Many options to make text and images on your phone easier to see

Lesson 6 - Accessibility (cont.)

  • Spoken Content - kind of like VoiceOver, but only reading the words on a webpage.
  • AssistiveTouch
    • helps if you have issues touching the screen
    • Provides a single button to help use your phone
  • Hearing Devices
    • Pair with hearing aids and sound processors
  • Add subtitles
  • And many more things!

Lesson 7 - Some Fun Stuff

  • Tips App
    • Can provide useful information about how to use your phone
  • Voice Dictation
    • On the onscreen keyboard, click the tiny microphone icon at the bottom right, and start talking to write instead of typing!
    • Can even recognize some formatting, like “new line”
  • Shazam built in to phone
    • Just ask Siri “What song is this?”
