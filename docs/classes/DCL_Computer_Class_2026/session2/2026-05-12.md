### Mouse Gestures (left vs right)
Mouse and touchpad have two main buttons:

- left mouse button and right mouse button
- Left typically for "selecting" and "activating"
- Right typically for "more options" / "context menu"
- Context = information associated with something

---

### Mouse Gestures (multiple modes of usage)
Left button has multiple ways you can use it

- Single click
- Double click
- Click and drag (hold button down while dragging)

---

### Mouse Gestures (touchpad)
 Touchpad works slightly different
 
- It still has two buttons at the bottom, but can:
-  use multiple fingers at the same time (2 or 3)
- click and  drag = double tap,  hold 2nd tap, then drag
- Pinch zoom in and out
- Google "Touchpad gestures windows 11" 
	- https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/learning-center/touchpad-gestures

---

### Mouse Gestures (modification keys)

- You can also modify the actions your mouse takes by using modifier keys, such as `Ctrl` and `Alt`
- I recommend you search what combinations do what, then test them out

### Scams
- What is a scam?
- 4 signs that something is likely a scam:
	- They **PRETEND** to be someone you know; gov't agency, local utility company, tech company, charity, etc.
	- There's a **PROBLEM** or **PRIZE**
	- **PRESSURE** to act immediately
	- They ask you to **PAY**  a certain way (cryptocurrency, wiring money, venmo/cashapp, gift card, etc.); they may actually send you a bad check but get you to send them money.
- Based on article from the [FTC](https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/how-avoid-scam)

---

### Scams (Cont'd)
> **Block unwanted calls and text messages.**
> **Don’t give your personal or financial information in response to a request that you didn’t expect.**
> **Resist the pressure to act immediately.**
> **Know how scammers tell you to pay.**
> **Stop and talk to someone you trust.**
